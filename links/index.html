If you are just learning mathematics, some of these sites might be of great help. (Naturally, the categories are not divising and will intersect; e.g., Milne or Scholze can be considered both geometers as number theorists, depending on your take.)

Number theory

  • Journal of Number Theory by Elsevier.
  • Journal de Théorie des Nombres de Bordeaux
  • Pete L. Clark
  • Keith Conrad
  • Natalia García-Fritz and Héctor Pastén
  • Bruno Kahn
  • Kiran S. Kedlaya
  • James S. Milne
  • Fabien Pazuki
  • Peter Scholze

Algebraic geometry

  • Stacks project – An ongoing open source project to write the most comprehensive text on algebraic geometry and, more concretely, on stacks.
  • EGA’s Table of Contents – The “article” Éléments de Géométrie Algébrique by A. Grothendieck was and is still a fundamental reference for algebraic geometry. Currently, all of its contents are spread throughtout the Stacks project, but it can be useful to go back to the original source.
  • Journal of Algebra by Elsevier.
  • Melvin Hochster
  • Pedro Sancho de Salas
  • Tamás Szamuely
  • Ravi Vakil
  • Vladimir Voedovsky

LaTeX

  • Bibliographies – Advice: Always prefer BibTeX over the embedded method!
  • Detexify – Find LaTeX symbols by drawing.
  • Everything I ever wanted to know about TikZ – A list of resources for learning how to use TikZ, including: a gallery of examples and the online manual.
  • quiver – A web tool for making commutative diagrams. More stable and better than Yichuan Shen’s editor.

Misceláneo

  • arXiv – A pre-publications site. Personally, I daily check the number theory, algebraic geometry and commutative algebra boards.
  • Numdam – An open archive of mathematical publications, specially by French mathematicians.
  • mathoverflow – Q&A board on mathematics. Warning: this is not the place for doing homework!
  • Mathematics Genealogy Project
  • MacTutor – Scientists biographies.
